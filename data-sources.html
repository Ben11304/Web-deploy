Data Sources

Multi-Hazard Energy Infrastructure Risk Analysis — April 2026

All sources are publicly available from U.S. federal agencies or open-data platforms.

Dataset Description Time Period Resolution Source / URL
Energy Assets
(OpenStreetMap)		 2,827,591 energy infrastructure assets: towers, generators, substations, plants, lines, fuel stations. Current (2024 extract) Point / Line / Polygon openstreetmap.org; ODbL license
Earthquake
(USGS NSHM 2023)		 Probabilistic PGA at 10% exceedance in 50 years (~475-yr return period), 50 states. Site class BC. 2023 model; 475-yr return period 0.2° contours Petersen et al. (2024) [1]; Data: 10.5066/P9GNPCOD
Flood
(FEMA NFHL)		 National Flood Hazard Layer: SFHA zone polygons (1% annual chance flood). Binary point-in-polygon classification. Regulatory; 1% annual chance (100-yr) Polygon (parcel-level) USA Flood Hazard Areas [3]; 44 CFR § 59.1
Wildfire
(USGS WFPI)		 Wildland Fire Potential Index: satellite-derived daily fire potential. P95 of daily rasters computed per pixel. 2008–2025 (daily) 1 km USGS EROS data server; Burgan et al. (1998) [4]
Hurricane
(NOAA IBTrACS v04r01)		 International Best Track Archive: tropical cyclone tracks, wind speed, wind radii. Wind swath reconstruction for exposure. 1842–2024 (183 years) 6-hourly track points; wind radii per quadrant Knapp et al. (2010) [5]; NCEI IBTrACS shapefile
Winter Storm
(NOAA SNODAS)		 Snow Data Assimilation System: daily gridded SWE. P95 of annual maximum SWE computed per pixel. 2003–2025 (daily) 1 km (CONUS) Barrett (2003) [6]; NSIDC download; DOI: 10.7265/N5TB14TC
Social Vulnerability
(CDC/ATSDR SVI)		 Social Vulnerability Index: county-level percentile ranking across 4 themes (socioeconomic, household, minority, housing). 2022 County-level CDC/ATSDR SVI 2022 [7]
Power Outage
(DOE EAGLE-I)		 County-level power outage event counts and customer impacts at 15-min intervals. 2022, 2024 County-level Tansakul et al. (2025) [8]; DOI: 10.13139/OLCF/2500278; ORNL
